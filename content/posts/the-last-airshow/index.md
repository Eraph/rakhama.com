Visiting airshows has been one of my favourite pastimes since my first visit to the now defunct [Leuchars Airshow](https://www.undiscoveredscotland.co.uk/leuchars/rafleucharsairshow/index.html) in the early 2000s. My love for aviation and flying goes back a bit further, likely emerging from playing some space/flight sims on the old [Atari ST](projects/atari-modernisation-project) such as [The Last Arcadian](https://www.atarilegend.com/games/the-last-arcadian), [Starglider 2](https://www.atarilegend.com/games/starglider-2) and [Carrier Command](https://www.atarilegend.com/games/carrier-command), as well as from assembling Airfix models of the [Red Arrow Gnat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folland_Gnat) and the [MiG-23](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikoyan-Gurevich_MiG-23).

It really took off when I boarded my first plane in 1995, as my family made the long journey from Aberdeen in the UK, to Perth in Australia, ready to begin our new life. That was it, I'd been in the air, I'd seen the world from up high and I was fascinated, completely hooked. I became obsessed with the [Ace Combat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ace_Combat) series of games which got me familiar with just about every modern fighter aircraft of the time, and I avidly built up an almost encyclopaedic knowledge of aircraft from my collection of [World Aircraft magazines](https://www.aeroflight.co.uk/mags/magazine-details/world-aircraft-information-files-magazine). Around the same time, Sky TV in the UK was broadcasting Discovery Wings, a channel entirely devoted to air and space flight. All of these served to fuel the obsession in me, and I eventually joined the [Air Training Corps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_Training_Corps).
<!--more-->
The Air Training Corps, also known as the Air Cadets, is a youth organisation not that dissimilar from Scouts or Boys Brigade. It obviously has a focus on aircraft and aviation, and is closely tied to the RAF. I'll stop short of calling it indoctrination - I for one learned valuable skills, took part in experiences I wouldn't otherwise have had the opportunity to do, and made lasting friendships in my time there.

It was with the Air Training Corps that I first visited the Leuchars Airshow. RAF Leuchars was an airbase just South of the Firth of Tay in Scotland.
